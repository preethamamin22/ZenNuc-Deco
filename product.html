Advanced Mechanism of Action & Kinetics Profile

A precisely sequenced biochemical cascade engineered to deliver absolute nucleic acid neutralisation.

Substrate Activation

Solution A (Deco Meta) delivers localised copper ions to the targeted biological grid, destabilising structural capsid envelopes and preparing exposed nucleic acids for catalytic degradation.

2

Solution B (Deco Rox) initiates a rapid Fenton-like reaction cascade, yielding powerful hydroxyl radicals right at the phosphodiester bonds, systematically fragmenting DNA and RNA chains.

Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) Workflow

For consistent validation within GLP-regulated environments, follow the certified sequential decontamination workflow outlined below.

1

Preparation

Clear target surfaces. Don standard PPE including nitrile gloves, lab coat, and safety eyewear before beginning the procedure.

2

Activation

Uniformly spray Solution A (Deco Meta), then follow immediately with Solution B (Deco Rox). Sequential order is critical to the reaction.

3

Incubation

Allow contact time of 2–5 minutes. A faint blue-green tint indicates chemical activation. Do not disturb during this phase.

4

Finalization

Wipe thoroughly using a lint-free tissue. Rinse with DI water or 70% Ethanol, then air dry. Surface is now fully decontaminated.

Scientific FAQs

Technical answers to the most common laboratory enquiries about ZenNuc™ Deco.

What makes ZenNuc™ Deco more effective than standard single-bottle decontaminants?
+

Single-bottle formulations suffer from continuous self-interaction of active ingredients, causing rapid shelf-degradation. ZenNuc™ Deco's dual-reagent system isolates Deco Meta and Deco Rox until the exact point of surface application, delivering a Cu²⁺-catalyzed Fenton-like reaction cascade with powerful hydroxyl radicals — achieving complete phosphodiester bond cleavage that single-bottle products cannot replicate.

Is ZenNuc™ Deco safe on PCR instruments and sensitive analytical equipment?
+

Yes. ZenNuc™ Deco is formulated as a non-corrosive system, safe on modern workspace polymers, glass, and alloy surfaces. Unlike traditional sodium hypochlorite-based cleaners — which are highly corrosive — ZenNuc™ Deco is gentle on PCR machines, biosafety cabinets, laminar flow hoods, pipettes, glass manifolds, and synthetic workstations.

What is the required contact time for effective decontamination?
+

The validated contact time is 2 to 5 minutes. After applying Solution A followed immediately by Solution B, allow the mixture to remain on the surface undisturbed for this period. The visible faint blue-green tint provides optical confirmation that the catalytic reaction is actively proceeding.

What does the blue-green colour change indicate?
+

The faint blue-green tint is a built-in visual activation indicator confirming the active degradation reaction is ongoing. It is generated by the copper-catalyzed peroxide cascade when Solution A and Solution B combine. This provides real-time confirmation that the decontamination chemistry is functioning correctly — no additional testing equipment needed.

How should Solution B (Deco Rox) be stored?
+

Solution B must be stored at ambient room temperature in a cool, dark place. Critically, do not decant Solution B into unshaded or clear secondary containers — light exposure triggers premature peroxide degradation, reducing efficacy. Always keep in the original opaque container.

Is ZenNuc™ Deco compatible with ISO-7 cleanrooms and BSL-3 facilities?
+

Yes. ZenNuc™ Deco has been tested and certified for seamless implementation across ISO-7 Cleanrooms and BSL-3 laboratory containment systems. The residue-free finalization protocol ensures complete safety for use within high-containment and ISO-classified environments.

Does every batch come with a Certificate of Analysis (CoA)?
+

Yes. Every batch of ZenNuc™ Deco undergoes extensive verification at our Mirjole M.I.D.C. manufacturing facility and is accompanied by a full Certificate of Analysis (CoA). This documentation supports GLP-compliant laboratory practices, audit trails, and regulatory submissions.

Can ZenNuc™ Deco be used in forensic DNA profiling laboratories?
+

Absolutely. ZenNuc™ Deco is validated for forensic laboratory environments where absolute clean-slate decontamination is critical. Complete destruction of DNA templates ensures evidence surfaces and processing workstations are free of any cross-contaminating nucleic acid residues that could compromise profiling accuracy.

What is the correct sequence for applying Solution A and Solution B?
+

The validated SOP: (1) Clear surfaces and don PPE. (2) Uniformly spray Solution A (Deco Meta) first. (3) Follow immediately with Solution B (Deco Rox). (4) Allow 2–5 minutes contact time. (5) Wipe with lint-free tissue. (6) Rinse with DI water or 70% Ethanol and air dry. Sequential order is critical — Solution A must precede Solution B.

Is ZenNuc™ Deco suitable for NGS library preparation areas?
+

Yes. ZenNuc™ Deco is validated to safeguard library preparation zones, flowcells, and sequencing instruments from residual amplicon contamination — preventing false sequences and protecting sequencing depth accuracy in NGS workflows.
